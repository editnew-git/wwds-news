[Worldwide Digital Signage](https://wwds.co) is preparing a public-facing school safety initiative designed to help schools display classroom clocks during normal operations and quickly switch to emergency alert messaging when urgent communication is required.

The browser-based system is being developed to support everyday classroom use while giving administrators and staff a clearer way to communicate safety responses across school displays. The initiative is planned as a donation-based rollout, with freeware-style access intended for schools that need a practical starting point without a complex software installation process.

<iframe src="https://samcmsuser.s3.amazonaws.com/camp/13930/index.html" width="100%" height="720" style="border:0; max-width:100%;" loading="lazy" title="Worldwide Digital Signage classroom safety alert system demo"></iframe>

Recent demonstrations have focused on a browser and QR-code controlled workflow, allowing schools to preview the alert experience quickly and understand how the system could operate in real classroom environments. Planned layouts include a standard no-alert classroom clock state along with multiple emergency alert options, enabling the same display to support routine timekeeping and urgent safety communication.

Worldwide Digital Signage is also preparing school-facing materials, a donation page, and training resources to help administrators, teachers, and support teams understand the alert workflow. The company is planning outreach to British Columbia schools and will review alignment opportunities with recognized school safety protocols and organizations, including the I Love U Guys Foundation and Make Our Schools Safe Foundation, once the school-facing layout is complete.

The project reflects Worldwide Digital Signage’s broader work in public-safety communication, including emergency messaging, missing-person alerts, disaster response, and digital signage override workflows.

The next step is to complete the school-facing materials, validate the demo against Hooria’s latest video, and prepare the initiative for review by schools and community safety stakeholders.
